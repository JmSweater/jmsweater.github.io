## 🛑 Project Overview: Solving the "Summer Comfort" Bug

In garment production, most brands treat summer development as a simple "Fiber Choice" (e.g., opting for 100% Cotton). However, from an engineering perspective, this is a **System Architecture Error**. 

Traditional knits fail in 85°F humidity because they lack an active cooling protocol. This project documents the transition from **Passive Material Selection** to **Active Hardware Optimization** using 18-Gauge (18GG) high-precision knitting.

---

## 🛠️ The Technology Stack

### **1. Low-Level Optimization: 18GG (The Micro-Processor)**
18-Gauge (18GG) isn't just a thinner knit; it is a high-density needle configuration. By increasing the "resolution" of the knit, we achieve:

* **Structural Integrity (The Debugged Fit):** Solves the common "sagging" bug. 18GG provides high-density support that maintains shape-memory, effectively extending the garment's lifecycle.
* **Micro-Ventilation Layers:** The ultra-fine needle grid creates an invisible micro-airflow buffer between the epidermis and the environment.

### **2. Stitch Engineering: Thermal Management Protocols**
We treat knitting patterns like **Execution Commands**. We move from passive fabrics to active "Hardware Overrides":

| Protocol | Technical Mechanism | Functional Output |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pointelle** | `Heat-Escape Command` | Architected physical openings that allow thermal energy to exit the system. |
| **Mesh Engineering** | `Active Circulation` | A specific geometric grid that converts the garment into an exhaust system. |

## 🌍 Sustainability Compliance (v2026)

For the 2026 deployment, **GRS-Certified (Global Recycled Standard)** fibers are the mandatory baseline. We apply an engineering mindset to sustainability:

* **Benchmarking:** We perform parity testing on moisture-wicking between recycled vs. virgin fibers.
* **Unit Testing:** Never push to production without a **5x Wash-Cycle Stress Test**. If the "code" (the stitch) breaks during laundering, the build is rejected.
