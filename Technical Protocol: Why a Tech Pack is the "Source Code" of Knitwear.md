## 🚩 The "Single Photo" Fallacy

In software, you can't compile a program from a screenshot. In knitwear, you cannot engineer a premium garment from a Pinterest photo. 

A photo represents **Inspiration**; a Tech Pack represents **Architecture**. Without the latter, the factory is forced to "guess" critical variables, leading to:
- `High Error Rate` in sampling.
- `Inconsistent Sizing` across production batches.
- `Structural Failure` (sagging, "shoulder horns," or shrinkage) after wash cycles.

---

## 📑 The Anatomy of a Professional Knitwear Tech Pack

At **JMSweater**, we require/provide the following "Technical Parameters" to ensure a 3-4 anti-pilling level and lifetime shape retention:

### 1. Yarn Configuration 🧶
| Parameter | Requirement | Why it matters |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Composition** | Detailed fiber blend % | Determines dye absorption and heat sensitivity. |
| **Yarn Count** | e.g., 2/26nm, 3/120s | Dictates machine tension and final garment weight. |

### 2. Point of Measure (POM) Matrix 📏
A professional blueprint must define more than just `Width` and `Length`. We calibrate:
- **Rib Heights & Tension:** To prevent cuff stretching.
- **Armhole Depth & Curve:** For ergonomic fit.
- **Neck Drop & Width:** To ensure structural integrity after 20+ washes.

### 3. Recovery & Stretch Coefficients
Knitwear is dynamic. We define the **Recovery Rate** to ensure that whether it is an **18GG ultra-fine knit** or a chunky **5GG cable knit**, the garment returns to its original silhouette after wear.
